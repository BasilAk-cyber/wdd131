Site Plan: Local Book Club

Site Name

Name: Local Book Club

Description: The name "Local Book Club" reflects a community-driven initiative to connect literature enthusiasts in our area. It is simple, memorable, and emphasizes inclusivity and local engagement. The logo features a stylized open book with a bookmark, symbolizing reading and community connection.

Domain Availability: localbookclub.org (hypothetical, not required for class).

Site Purpose

The Local Book Club website serves as a hub for book lovers to explore a curated reading list, join discussions and events, and become members. It provides information on upcoming books, activities (e.g., author talks, workshops), and a membership form to foster community engagement. The site aims to promote literary appreciation and build connections among readers.

Scenarios

The website targets book enthusiasts, including casual readers and avid bibliophiles, seeking to join a local reading community. Below are two scenarios representing questions from the target audience:

  • What books are scheduled for discussion, and when are the meetings?
  • How can I join the book club and receive updates on events?

Color Schema

The color schema ensures a clean and engaging design:

  • Primary Color (white, #FFFFFF): Used for page backgrounds and header/footer to create a clean, neutral base.
  • Secondary Color (black, #000000): Applied to text, hero banner, and section headings for high contrast.
  • Accent Color 1 (#FFD166, yellow): Used for buttons and hover states to draw attention.
  • Accent Color 2 (#F7F7F7, light gray): Used for subtle backgrounds in cards and forms.

Typography

The site uses two Google Fonts for a modern look:

  • Montserrat (Bold 700): Used for headings (h1, h2, h3) to create a bold, professional appearance.
  • Open Sans (Regular): Used for body text, form labels, and paragraphs for readability.
